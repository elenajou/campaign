for Chair of Downtown Campus

Campaign Proposal

Introduction

Hi, I’m Elena Jou, a Term 3 CST student in the co-op program with experience in the tech industry. Coming from the Burnaby campus, I’ve seen firsthand how different the student experience is for Downtown students. We have talented, hardworking students, but we lack the same career opportunities, networking events, and campus support as I’ve seen in Burnaby. That’s why I’m running, to ensure we get the resources, recognition, and opportunities we deserve.

I am passionate about ensuring Downtown Campus students receive the same recognition and resources as Burnaby students.

Campaign Strategy

If elected, I will:

  		•  Bring career fairs and networking events to Downtown Campus.
    I will push for on-campus career fairs and employer meetups so students don’t have to travel to Burnaby. These events will provide opportunities for students to connect with industry professionals and learn about job openings.
  		•  Introduce BCIT Network Hub for structured speed-friending and networking.
    This event will help students build friendships and professional connections in a relaxed environment. It will focus on connecting students across various disciplines and building a strong campus community.
  		•  Organize pre-exam wellness events featuring therapy dogs, meditation, and art therapy.
    These wellness events will help reduce stress before exams and foster a supportive, positive environment on campus. Students can relax, meditate, and connect with therapy animals to improve their mental health.
  		•  Negotiate discounts for Downtown students with local businesses.
    I will work with local businesses to provide discounts for students on essentials like food, fitness, and study-friendly spaces. This will help reduce financial stress, especially in the high-cost area of Downtown Vancouver.
  		•  Advocate for more visibility and financial support for student clubs.
    Student clubs at Downtown campus need more recognition and resources. I will advocate for better funding and visibility for all clubs to foster engagement and enhance student life.

In-Depth Campaign Plan

My campaign focuses on:

  		•  Career Fairs & Networking: Monthly 'Lunch & Launch' industry meetups.
    By hosting industry professionals for lunch sessions, students can casually meet employers and explore career opportunities without the need to travel to Burnaby.
  		•  Student Perks: 'Downtown Deals for Students' program offering discounts with local businesses.
    The program will include discounts for everyday essentials, making student life in Downtown more affordable.
  		•  Promoting Community: Organizing speed-friending events and social mixers.
    These events will give students the opportunity to connect, make friends, and create a supportive, collaborative campus environment.
  		•  Wellness Support: BCIT Chill Week with therapy dogs and stress-relief activities.
    I will introduce pre-exam events focused on mental well-being to help students relax, destress, and prepare for exams with a clear mind.

Q&A

What is the Chair of Downtown Campus and what do they do?

The Chair of Downtown Campus represents the voice of BCIT's Downtown students, ensuring our needs are heard by the administration and creating opportunities for professional and social growth.

How am I going to make a change?

I will create programs like 'BCIT Downtown Pulse' for feedback, 'Lunch & Launch' for networking, and 'Downtown Deals for Students' for discounts. I will work to bridge the gap between Downtown and Burnaby campuses.
